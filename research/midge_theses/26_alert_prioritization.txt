**Chapter 2: Background and Related Work**

Effective alert prioritization is a well-studied problem in systems management and operations research. Traditional approaches to alert prioritization often rely on static rules and pre-defined thresholds. These rule-based systems assign severity levels to alerts based on the type of event and the affected system component. While simple to implement, rule-based systems often lack the flexibility to adapt to changing system conditions and fail to account for the complex interdependencies between different components.

Statistical methods offer a more dynamic approach to alert prioritization. These methods analyze historical alert data to identify patterns and anomalies. By comparing current alert characteristics to historical trends, statistical methods can detect unusual events that might warrant higher priority. For example, a sudden increase in the frequency of a particular type of alert might indicate a developing problem that requires immediate attention.

Machine learning techniques have emerged as a powerful tool for alert prioritization. Supervised learning algorithms can be trained on labeled data to predict the severity or urgency of an alert based on its characteristics. Unsupervised learning algorithms can be used to cluster alerts into different categories, allowing operations teams to focus on the most critical clusters. Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques can analyze the content of alert messages to extract relevant information and identify potential root causes. The challenge of machine learning approaches is the need for labelled training data.

Context-aware computing considers various aspects of the environment to improve decision-making. In the context of alert prioritization, this includes factors such as the time of day, the current system load, the status of related systems, and the business impact of potential disruptions.
