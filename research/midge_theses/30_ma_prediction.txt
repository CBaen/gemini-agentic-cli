Mergers and acquisitions represent significant events in the corporate world, often leading to substantial financial gains for those who can anticipate them. Insider trading, the buying or selling of securities based on non-public information, is illegal but unfortunately prevalent around M&A announcements. This thesis posits that by analyzing patterns in insider trading activity, particularly through the lens of advanced AI models like Gemini, one can gain a predictive edge in forecasting M&A events.

The MIDGE system, hypothetically designed to track insider trades, forms the basis for this research. **Chapter 2: Literature Review**

A thorough review of existing literature reveals strong evidence supporting the link between insider trading and M&A activity. Studies have shown that:

*   Unusual trading patterns by insiders, especially in equity options, often precede M&A announcements. These patterns are characterized by abnormal trading volumes and increased implied volatility.
*   Insiders in acquiring firms may adjust their trading strategies around M&A announcements, increasing net purchases in deals with strong asset fit.
*   Higher levels of insider trading (specifically, a greater net purchase ratio by insiders) in the target company during the year leading up to the announcement correlate with improved synergy and higher returns for the acquiring firm.

Machine learning techniques are increasingly used to detect insider trading. Anomaly detection, decision trees, clustering, social network analysis, and deep learning models are all employed to identify suspicious trading behaviors. NLP and text mining can be used to analyze communications and public data for M&A signals.

This chapter explores how Gemini can be utilized to detect M&A signals from insider activity.

*   **Pattern Recognition:** Gemini can be trained on vast datasets of historical insider trading data and M&A announcements to identify patterns that distinguish routine trading from trading based on acquisition knowledge. These patterns might include:
    *   Sudden increases in trading volume by specific insiders.
    *   Clusters of insiders buying shares in the same company.
    *   Unusual option activity, such as the purchase of out-of-the-money call options.
    *   Trading activity that deviates significantly from an insider's historical trading behavior.
*   **Contextual Analysis:** Gemini can analyze news articles, social media posts, and company filings to gather contextual information that might support the M&A hypothesis. For example, rumors of a potential acquisition, a company's financial performance, and industry trends can all be factored into the analysis.
*   **Sentiment Analysis:** Sentiment analysis can be used to gauge market sentiment towards a company. Positive sentiment could increase the likelihood of an acquisition.
